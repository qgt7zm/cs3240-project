Mental Health Resources for Student-Athletes

Welcome to our resource page dedicated to supporting the mental well-being of student-athletes. Here you'll find a range of materials and contacts that can assist you in managing the unique challenges faced by athletes.

It's crucial to prioritize and organize. Utilize planners and scheduling apps to block out time for studying, training, and rest. Don't hesitate to talk to your coaches and teachers about your schedule so they can provide support. Remember, taking breaks and self-care activities are just as important as your academic and athletic commitments.

Feeling overwhelmed is a signal to seek support. Speak with a counselor, a sports psychologist, or a trusted mentor. Schools often provide resources for student-athletes to manage their mental health. Additionally, make sure you're getting enough rest, eating healthily, staying hydrated, and maintaining social connections.

It's very important to have a balance. Social interactions can provide emotional support and stress relief. Engaging in social activities can recharge your mind and improve your overall happiness.

Post-competition recovery is as crucial as the preparation. It includes active recovery, proper nutrition, hydration, sleep, and mental rest. Techniques such as meditation, yoga, and even hobbies outside sports can help in psychological recovery.

Performance anxiety can be managed by developing a consistent pre-performance routine, focusing on process goals instead of outcomes, and practicing mindfulness. Also, speak openly about your feelings with a coach or psychologist.

Nutrition has a significant impact on mental health. A diet rich in vitamins, minerals, and antioxidants is vital for cognitive function and mood regulation. Conversely, a poor diet can lead to fatigue, impaired decision-making, and increased stress.

If you're experiencing mental health issues, it's crucial to seek professional help. Here are some contacts:

  • Campus Counseling Center: Available to all students for confidential consultations.
  • National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357), a confidential, free, 24-hour-a-day service.
  • SAMHSA’s National Helpline: 1-800-662-HELP (4357).
  • Mental Health America: Support and resources for mental health.
  • Find a Therapist: Search for local mental health professionals.
  • BetterHelp: Access to licensed, trained, experienced, and accredited psychologists.

Learn more about mental health with these resources:

  • MentalHealth.gov: Information on various mental health disorders.
  • NCAA Sport Science Institute: Mental health educational resources.
  • Mind: Mental health information and support.
  • Headspace: Tools and resources for mindfulness and meditation.
  • TED Talks on Mental Health: Inspiring talks about mental health and well-being.
  • CDC Mental Health: Provides information on mental health and how to improve it.
  • National Institute of Mental Health: The largest scientific organization dedicated to mental health research.

Connecting with other student-athletes can provide invaluable support. Here are some communities:

  • Athletes for Hope: A network for athletes to share experiences.
  • Student Athlete Health Initiative: A platform for student-athletes to share experiences and support each other's mental health.
  • Athletes Connected: A program dedicated to supporting the mental health of college athletes through outreach, education, and research.
  • JED Campus: A nationwide initiative designed to help colleges and universities support student mental health and prevent substance abuse and suicide.
  • Next College Student Athlete (NCSA): Provides resources and guidance for student-athletes navigating the college recruiting process while prioritizing mental health and well-being.
  • Active for Life: Offers mental health tips and resources specifically tailored for young athletes.

In an emergency, it's important to get immediate help:

  • Emergency Services: Call 911 for immediate assistance.
  • Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741 for free, 24/7 crisis counseling.
